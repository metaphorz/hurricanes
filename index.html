Multi-Hurricane Track Visualization

This interactive visualization displays the tracks of seven significant hurricanes with color-coded intensity segments based on the Saffir-Simpson hurricane scale.

View Interactive Visualization

Featured Hurricanes

Hurricane Irene (2011)
Category 3

Active: August 21-30, 2011

Irene caused widespread damage across the Caribbean and along the U.S. East Coast, particularly in Vermont where it caused catastrophic flooding.

Hurricane Andrew (1992)
Category 5

Active: August 16-28, 1992

One of the most destructive hurricanes to hit the United States, causing major damage in the Bahamas, Florida, and Louisiana.

Hurricane Ian (2022)
Category 4

Active: September 22 - October 1, 2022

Ian caused catastrophic damage in western Cuba and the southeast United States, especially in Florida and South Carolina.

Hurricane Ida (2021)
Category 4

Active: August 26 - September 4, 2021

Ida was the second-most damaging hurricane to strike Louisiana, behind only Hurricane Katrina, and caused catastrophic flooding in the Northeastern United States.

Hurricane Milton (2024)
Category 5

Active: October 4-11, 2024

Milton rapidly intensified into a powerful Category 5 hurricane in the Gulf of Mexico before making landfall in Florida, causing extensive damage.

Hurricane Idalia (2023)
Category 4

Active: August 26 - September 8, 2023

Idalia caused significant damage in Florida's Big Bend region after rapidly intensifying in the Gulf of Mexico.

Hurricane Helene (2024)
Category 4

Active: September 23-28, 2024

Helene caused catastrophic flooding and damage across the southeastern United States, particularly in North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, and Tennessee.

About the Visualization

This interactive visualization allows you to:

  • View all seven hurricane tracks simultaneously on an interactive map
  • See color-coded line segments representing hurricane intensity categories
  • Hover over track segments to see basic information
  • Click on any hurricane track or marker for detailed information
  • Use the scrollable information panel to see a summary of all hurricanes

Project Details

This visualization was created using:

  • Python for data processing
  • Folium (based on Leaflet.js) for interactive mapping
  • Data from the International Best Track Archive for Climate Stewardship (IBTrACS)

For more information about this project, including source code and technical details, visit the GitHub repository.
